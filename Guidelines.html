Guidelines

The New Zealand Junior Science Journal invites submissions of Core Concept papers or Independent Investigation reports with a length of two to five pages (excluding references). Submissions are accepted across two age divisions: Years 7–8 and Years 9–10.

All reports must be prepared using the appropriate template linked below, depending on the type of research being submitted. Authors are also encouraged to read the Writing Reports Guide before beginning. Figures should appear within the paper; however, each figure must also be uploaded separately along with its caption text.

For the 2026–2027 Spring edition, submissions must be received by May 31st, 2026. Please note that the submission portal for this edition will open on March 1st, 2026.

Formatting Specifications

  • Length: 2–5 pages (references and endnotes not included)
  • Font: Times New Roman
  • Font size: 10 pt
  • Layout: Use official NZJSJ templates
  • Margins: Template margins must remain unchanged
  • Writing style: Formal scientific language
  • Spelling and grammar must be accurate and consistent

Files should be named using the format below to ensure correct processing:
LastnameFirstname_paper.docx, LastnameFirstname_figures.ppt

Templates and Guide

All authors are required to use the correct submission template. We strongly recommend reviewing the Writing Reports Guide prior to writing, as it outlines expectations and provides detailed advice for successful submissions.

  • NZJSJ Writing Reports Guide (PDF)
  • Core Concept Template (.docx)
  • Independent Investigation Template (.docx)
  • Figures Template (.ppt)
